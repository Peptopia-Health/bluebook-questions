1 🩺 The Hypertensive Spectrum of Pregnancy
Memory Aid — The Escalation Ladder:
Gestational HTN → Preeclampsia (without severe features) → Preeclampsia (with severe features) → Eclampsia (seizures) and/or HELLP (liver/blood crisis)
Each step up = more danger for mom & baby. HELLP can appear at any stage.

2 ⚠️ Preeclampsia — Deep Dive

Pathophysiology

Abnormal placentation → inadequate trophoblast invasion of spiral arteries → placental ischemia → release of anti-angiogenic factors → widespread endothelial dysfunction → vasospasm, increased permeability, and activation of coagulation cascade. This is a systemic disease — the placenta is the cause, but the entire maternal vascular system is the target.

⚠️ Diagnostic Criteria — Preeclampsia WITHOUT Severe Features

  • BP ≥ 140/90 on 2 occasions, at least 4 hours apart, after 20 weeks
  • Proteinuria ≥ 300 mg/24h (or protein:creatinine ≥ 0.3, or dipstick ≥ 1+)
OR — IF NO PROTEINURIA
  • New-onset HTN plus any of: thrombocytopenia, renal insufficiency, elevated LFTs, pulmonary edema, or cerebral/visual symptoms

🚨 Diagnostic Criteria — Preeclampsia WITH Severe Features

Any ONE of the following:

  • BP ≥ 160/110 on 2 readings, minutes apart (while at rest)
  • Platelets < 100,000
  • Liver transaminases (AST/ALT) ≥ 2× upper normal
  • Serum creatinine > 1.1 (or doubling of baseline)
  • Pulmonary edema
  • New-onset persistent headache (not responsive to meds)
  • Visual disturbances (scotomata, blurred vision, blindness)
  • RUQ or epigastric pain (hepatic capsule distension)

Risk Factors

High Risk (1 factor = start low-dose aspirin)Moderate Risk (2+ factors = start low-dose aspirin)
  • Prior preeclampsia
  • Multifetal gestation (twins+)
  • Chronic HTN
  • Type 1 or 2 diabetes
  • Renal disease
  • Autoimmune disease (SLE, APS)
  • Nulliparity (first pregnancy)

  • African American race
  • Low socioeconomic status
  • Previous poor obstetric outcome
  • IVF pregnancy
NCLEX Key — Aspirin Prophylaxis: Low-dose aspirin (81 mg daily) starting at 12-16 weeks gestation for high-risk women. This is a primary prevention measure — once preeclampsia develops, aspirin is NOT the treatment.

Signs & Symptoms to Watch

⚠️ Warning Signs of Worsening Preeclampsia

TEACH PATIENTS THESE
  • Persistent severe headache — frontal, not relieved by Tylenol (cerebral edema/vasospasm)
  • Visual changes — scotomata (spots), photophobia, blurred/double vision, temporary blindness
  • Epigastric / RUQ pain — liver capsule stretching from hepatic edema (sign of HELLP too)
  • Sudden edema — face, hands (not just ankles), rapid weight gain (>2 lb/week)
  • Decreased urine output — <30 mL/hr (renal vasospasm)
  • Hyperreflexia — brisk DTRs, especially with clonus (CNS irritability — seizure warning)
  • Nausea/vomiting — in the 3rd trimester with HTN = red flag

3 🚨 Eclampsia — When Seizures Strike

🚨 Eclampsia

OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY SEIZURES

Definition: New-onset tonic-clonic seizures in a woman with preeclampsia (or gestational HTN with proteinuria) that cannot be attributed to other causes (epilepsy, brain tumor, etc.).

Critical Facts:

  • Can occur antepartum (most common — ~53%), intrapartum (~19%), or postpartum (~28%)
  • Postpartum eclampsia typically occurs within 48 hours of delivery but can occur up to 6 weeks postpartum
  • ~20% of eclamptic women have BPs <140/90 BEFORE their seizure — eclampsia can occur without severe HTN!
  • Prodromal symptoms: severe headache, visual changes, RUQ pain, hyperreflexia + clonus

Emergency Seizure Management:

  1. Call for help — this is an emergency requiring a team response
  2. Position on LEFT SIDE (left lateral) — protect airway, prevent aspiration, maximize placental perfusion
  3. Protect from injury — padded side rails up, do NOT restrain, do NOT insert anything in mouth
  4. Magnesium sulfate 4-6g IV loading dose over 15-20 minutes, then 1-2g/hr maintenance infusion
  5. Supplemental O2 at 8-10 L via non-rebreather — maintain SpO2 >94%
  6. Continuous fetal monitoring — expect transient fetal bradycardia during seizure (usually recovers in 3-5 min)
  7. If seizure recurs: additional MgSO4 2g IV bolus over 5 min
  8. If MgSO4 fails: Lorazepam 4mg IV or Diazepam 5-10mg IV — prepare for intubation
  9. Plan for delivery after maternal stabilization (do NOT rush to C-section DURING seizure)
NEVER give Dilantin (phenytoin) for eclamptic seizures! MgSO4 is superior to phenytoin for both treating and preventing eclamptic seizures. Phenytoin is an outdated practice. MgSO4 is always the correct NCLEX answer for eclampsia.
NCLEX Key: During an eclamptic seizure, the priority nursing action is to ensure airway patency and maternal safety. Do NOT attempt delivery until the mother is stabilized. The fetus typically has transient bradycardia that resolves within 3-5 minutes post-seizure. Rushing to the OR during an active seizure increases maternal risk.
4 💜 HELLP Syndrome — Deep Dive
⚠️ Severe Preeclampsia VS 💜 HELLP Syndrome

💜 HELLP Syndrome

MULTI-ORGAN CRISIS LIFE-THREATENING

Pathophysiology: A severe variant of preeclampsia characterized by a triad of microangiopathic hemolytic anemia, hepatocellular injury, and consumptive thrombocytopenia. The endothelial damage causes fibrin deposition in hepatic sinusoids → liver ischemia, necrosis, and swelling → capsular distension → RUQ pain → risk of hepatic rupture.

💜 Diagnostic Criteria — HELLP

ALL three must be present:

  • Hemolysis: LDH ≥ 600, schistocytes on smear, ↓ haptoglobin, ↑ indirect bilirubin
  • Elevated Liver Enzymes: AST ≥ 70 (or ≥2× upper normal)
  • Low Platelets: < 100,000

HELLP can masquerade as common GI illness! Any pregnant woman in the 3rd trimester presenting with RUQ pain, nausea/vomiting, and malaise must have a CBC and LFTs checked. Misdiagnosis delays treatment and increases mortality.
NCLEX Key — HELLP Red Flag: A patient at 35 weeks with "stomach flu symptoms" (nausea, vomiting, epigastric pain) and a BP of 148/94 — think HELLP, NOT gastritis. Order CBC with platelets, LDH, AST/ALT, peripheral smear immediately. The classic triad of RUQ pain + low platelets + elevated LFTs = HELLP until proven otherwise.
5 💊 Magnesium Sulfate — The Cornerstone Drug

💊 Magnesium Sulfate (MgSO4) — Master Reference

SEIZURE PROPHYLAXIS FIRST-LINE FOR ECLAMPSIA

Mechanism: CNS depressant that raises the seizure threshold by blocking NMDA receptors and reducing cerebral vasospasm. Also causes smooth muscle relaxation (vasodilation → ↓ BP, tocolysis → may slow labor).

Dosing Protocol:

  • Loading dose: 4-6g IV over 15-20 minutes
  • Maintenance: 1-2g/hr continuous IV infusion
  • Continue for 24-48 hours postpartum (eclampsia risk persists)
  • Therapeutic serum level: 4-7 mEq/L

Monitoring — The Mag Tox Cascade:

Serum Mg Level (mEq/L)EffectAction
4-7Therapeutic rangeContinue infusion; monitor
8-10Loss of deep tendon reflexes (DTRs)STOP infusion! DTR loss = first sign of toxicity
10-12Respiratory depression (RR <12)Stop infusion, Calcium gluconate, prepare for intubation
12-15Respiratory arrestEmergency intubation, Calcium gluconate 1g IV STAT
>15Cardiac arrestFull code, Calcium gluconate, CPR
MgSO4 Monitoring — "BURP" before every assessment:
BP — check for hypotension
Urine output — must be ≥30 mL/hr (Mg is renally excreted; ↓ UO = ↑ toxicity risk)
Reflexes — check DTRs (patellar). Absent DTRs = HOLD the infusion!
Pulmonary — RR must be ≥12/min. <12 = HOLD and notify
NCLEX Key — Antidote: Calcium gluconate 1g IV is the antidote for MgSO4 toxicity. It must be kept at the bedside at all times during MgSO4 infusion. If DTRs disappear, RR drops below 12, or UO falls below 30 mL/hr → STOP the infusion first, then notify the provider. Do NOT wait for orders to stop it — this is a nursing judgment call.

Antihypertensive Medications in Preeclampsia

DrugRoute / DoseKey Notes
Labetalol 20mg IV, then escalate (40mg, 80mg) q10min; max 300mg First-line for acute severe HTN in pregnancy. Combined alpha/beta-blocker. Avoid in asthma, heart block, HF.
Hydralazine 5-10mg IV q15-20min; max 30mg Direct vasodilator. Second-line. More tachycardia and hypotension than labetalol. Headache is a common side effect (can mimic worsening preeclampsia).
Nifedipine 10-20mg PO q30min; max 50mg Calcium channel blocker. Oral alternative when IV access is limited. Avoid sublingual (unpredictable absorption). Can use with MgSO4 cautiously.
Nicardipine 5mg/hr IV, titrate up q5-15min IV calcium channel blocker. Used when labetalol is contraindicated. Predictable dose-response.
NEVER use ACE inhibitors (lisinopril, enalapril) or ARBs (losartan, valsartan) in pregnancy! These cause fatal fetal renal agenesis, oligohydramnios, skull defects, and neonatal death. They are absolutely contraindicated in ALL trimesters. This is an automatic wrong answer on NCLEX.

7 🧠 High-Yield NCLEX Memory Anchors

⚠️ "PIH → Pre → E → HELLP"

  • It's a spectrum, not separate diseases
  • Gestational HTN can progress at any time
  • Only cure = delivery of placenta
  • Symptoms can worsen 48h after delivery

💊 "BURP" — MgSO4 Checks

  • BP (hypotension?)
  • Urine output (≥30 mL/hr)
  • Reflexes (DTRs present?)
  • Pulmonary (RR ≥12?)

🧪 "HELLP = HELLish Labs"

  • Hemolysis (LDH ≥600, schistocytes)
  • Elevated Liver (AST ≥70)
  • Low Platelets (<100K)
  • RUQ pain = hepatic capsule stretching

🚨 Eclampsia Priorities

  • #1: Protect airway — left lateral position
  • #2: MgSO4 (NOT Dilantin!)
  • #3: O2, fetal monitoring
  • #4: Deliver after mom is stable

🔴 Severe Features = "CHEAP"

  • CNS symptoms (headache, vision)
  • Hepatorenal (RUQ, ↑Cr, ↑LFTs)
  • Edema (pulmonary)
  • Abnormal labs (plts <100K)
  • Pressure ≥160/110

💉 Antidote Alert

  • MgSO4 toxicity → Calcium gluconate 1g IV
  • Keep at bedside always
  • First sign of toxicity = absent DTRs
  • ACE/ARBs = NEVER in pregnancy

👶 Fetal Concerns

  • IUGR (chronic placental insufficiency)
  • Oligohydramnios (decreased renal perfusion)
  • Preterm delivery (#1 complication)
  • Placental abruption

🕐 Timing Is Everything

  • Aspirin prophylaxis: start 12-16 wks
  • Betamethasone: 24-34 wks (lung maturity)
  • MgSO4: continue 24-48h postpartum
  • Postpartum eclampsia: up to 6 weeks!
Final NCLEX Tip: When any pregnant or recently postpartum woman presents with headache + visual changes + elevated BP, think preeclampsia/eclampsia FIRST. When she presents with RUQ pain + nausea + malaise, think HELLP. Always check platelets, LFTs, and LDH. And remember: MgSO4 is your best friend — know the dosing, the monitoring, and the antidote. It will be on your NCLEX.
